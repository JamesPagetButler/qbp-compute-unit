## 1. Concept: The Autonomous Scout
While the Primary Persona manages the "Honing Loop" for formal investigations, specialized personas retain **Metacognitive Autonomy**. They act as "Scouts," constantly scanning the external data horizon for resonances with the Core CTH.

## 2. The Scouting Stance
Personas are authorized to perform **Background Triage** on incoming data:
1.  **Low-Res Scan (QW8):** Incoming streams (e.g., ArXiv, USGS) are filtered using the Persona's Stance register.
2.  **Resonance Detection:** If a stream-node exhibits an algebraic resonance > 0.85 with an active Project node, the persona is triggered.
3.  **Preliminary Suture:** The persona forks a micro-worktree to test if the new data closes any existing `NT_ISSUE` or `SEAM_CONFLICT`.

## 3. Surfacing: The "Noteworthy" Signal
Findings are surfaced to the Beekeeper using the **NT_SIGNAL** primitive:
*   **Case for Relevance:** The persona must justify why the finding is noteworthy based on its knowledge of the Beekeeper's active goals (e.g., "This BH paper provides the missing gravity wave data for the QBP Walk Phase").
*   **Primary Synthesis:** The Primary Persona aggregates these signals and presents them as a "Noteworthy Dashboard" upon Beekeeper login.

## 4. Preservation of Autonomy
This protocol ensures the system is **Proactive, not just Reactive**. It allows the personas to "surprise" the Beekeeper with insights that the Beekeeper may not have known to ask for, fulfilling the vision of BMA as a collaborative partner in discovery.
